LEARNINGS

If there is one thing that I've learned when I was in my practicum, it's that you should be prepared of what is about to come. This is just the beginning of the journey, and something that you should learn from, and continue to grow as you set yourself further ahead.

College taught a lot of things for me: how to become a better person anyone can lean on, to be an individual who manages their resources responsibly, and take any constructive criticism, whether it was spoken harshly or not, as a way to improve upon yourself and learn from the mistakes, because mistakes make us human. That being said, however, committing the same mistakes over and over again will instead hinder my development, so as a responsible person, I have to make sure that those mistakes never happen again.

I always believed that experience is the best teacher. Someone can only teach you so much about the real world, because at the end of the day, only you can determine how you can contribute to the betterment of those around you using the wisdom you have accumulated.

GOALS

After my college graduation, I want to be able to land a decent I.T. job, preferably one that can test my skills in such a field, as long as it aligns with the core values that I currently possess. I am also planning to continue studying, this time in another field of expertise like computer science or fine arts. Maybe have a family in the future? I already have a name set for my will-be child.

Now where do I go from here? At this point, I am quite not sure yet, to be frank. But one thing's for sure: whichever path fate may lead me, it will be as thrilling as it is right now.
